# Shahnameh Complete Character Timeline & Dependencies

## Overview
The Shahnameh spans approximately 3,000+ years of Persian mythology and history, divided into three major eras:
- **Mythical Era**: Creation to establishment of kingship (~7,000 years in mythology)
- **Heroic Era**: The great dynasties and legendary heroes (~1,000 years)
- **Historical Era**: Semi-historical Sasanian period to Arab conquest (~400 years)

---

## ERA 1: MYTHICAL/PISHDADIAN DYNASTY (Dawn of Time - ~1000 BCE)

### Generation 1: Primordial Kings

**KEYUMARS (First King, First Human)**
- Timeline: Beginning of creation
- Role: First king, taught humans civilization
- Relations: Grandfather of Hushang
- Story Arc: Golden age, death by Ahriman's demons
- Game Potential: Tutorial era, establishing world rules

**HUSHANG (The Lawgiver)**
- Timeline: After Keyumars
- Role: Discovered fire, established laws
- Relations: Grandson of Keyumars, father of Tahmuras
- Story Arc: Civilization builder, inventor
- Dependencies: None (can standalone)

**TAHMURAS (The Binder)**
- Timeline: After Hushang
- Role: Conquered demons (divs), learned writing from them
- Relations: Son of Hushang, father of Jamshid
- Story Arc: First great warrior-king
- Key Events: Binding Ahriman, learning demonic secrets

### Generation 2: Golden Age & The Fall

**JAMSHID (The Magnificent)**
- Timeline: ~500 year reign
- Role: Greatest early king, brought golden age
- Relations: Son of Tahmuras, predecessor to Zahhak
- Story Arc: Pride leads to downfall
- Key Events: 
  - Created Nowruz (New Year)
  - Built Persepolis
  - Became arrogant, lost divine favor
  - Fled from Zahhak
- Dependencies: His fall enables Zahhak's rise
- **CRITICAL CHARACTER**: His disappearance creates power vacuum

**ZAHHAK (The Serpent King) ‚öîÔ∏è**
- Timeline: 1,000 year tyranny
- Role: Primary villain of mythical era
- Relations: Son of Arab king Mardas, killer of Jamshid
- Story Arc: Deceived by Ahriman, grew serpents from shoulders
- Key Events:
  - Killed his father
  - Serpents fed with human brains daily
  - Imprisoned by Fereydun
- Dependencies: Needs Jamshid's fall, creates context for Fereydun
- **BOSS CHARACTER**: Multi-generational antagonist

**KAVA THE BLACKSMITH**
- Timeline: During Zahhak's reign
- Role: Revolutionary leader, common hero
- Relations: His sons sacrificed to Zahhak
- Story Arc: Started rebellion with leather apron (Derafsh Kaviani)
- Key Events: Created Persian battle standard
- Dependencies: Simultaneous with Fereydun's rise
- **PEOPLE'S HERO**: Represents common folk vs tyranny

### Generation 3: Liberation

**FEREYDUN (The Just King)**
- Timeline: Defeated Zahhak ~750 BCE (mythical)
- Role: Liberator, wise king
- Relations: Descendant of Jamshid, father of Salm, Tur, and Iraj
- Story Arc: Heroic rise, tragic family division
- Key Events:
  - Raised in secret in mountains
  - Wielded ox-headed mace (gurz)
  - Imprisoned Zahhak in Mt. Damavand
  - Divided world among three sons
- Dependencies: Requires Zahhak as antagonist
- Children: Salm, Tur (villains), Iraj (hero-martyr)
- **LEGENDARY HERO**: Justice incarnate

**SALM & TUR (The Fratricides)**
- Timeline: Fereydun's era
- Role: Jealous brothers, murderers
- Relations: Older sons of Fereydun, killers of Iraj
- Story Arc: Envy leads to murder, starts cycle of revenge
- Dependencies: Simultaneous with Iraj
- Consequence: Their murder creates Iran-Turan conflict

**IRAJ (The Martyr Prince)**
- Timeline: Same as brothers
- Role: Innocent victim, youngest son
- Relations: Son of Fereydun, favorite child
- Story Arc: Given best lands (Iran), murdered by jealous brothers
- Dependencies: Father's decision causes tragedy
- **MARTYRDOM**: His death drives centuries of conflict

**MANUCHEHR**
- Timeline: Grandson of Iraj
- Role: Avenger, rightful king of Iran
- Relations: Iraj's grandson, defeats Salm & Tur
- Story Arc: Restores justice, avenges grandfather
- Dependencies: Requires Iraj's death
- Consequence: Establishes lasting Iran-Turan enmity

## ERA 2: HEROIC/KAYANID DYNASTY (~1000-300 BCE)

### The Eternal Hero

**ROSTAM (The Champion of Champions) ‚öîÔ∏è**
- Timeline: Lives through 7 generations of kings
- Role: Supreme hero, champion of Iran
- Relations: 
  - Father: Zal (raised by Simurgh)
  - Mother: Rudaba (princess of Kabul)
  - Son: Sohrab (killed by Rostam unknowingly)
  - Brother: Zavareh
  - Grandfather: Sam (great warrior)
- Story Arc: Birth to death spans centuries
- Key Events:
  - **The Seven Trials (Haft Khan)**
  - Rescue of Kay Kavus (multiple times)
  - Killing of Sohrab (greatest tragedy)
  - Wars against Turan
  - Death in pit trap by brother Shaghad
- Dependencies: Central to almost all heroic era stories
- **PLAYABLE PROTAGONIST**: Multi-generational perspective character

**SAM (The Warrior)**
- Timeline: Before Rostam
- Role: Great warrior, Rostam's grandfather
- Relations: Father of Zal, grandfather of Rostam
- Story Arc: Abandons albino son Zal, later reconciles
- Key Events: Rejects then accepts Zal
- Dependencies: Establishes Rostam's lineage

**ZAL (The White-Haired)**
- Timeline: Father's generation before Rostam
- Role: Raised by Simurgh (phoenix), Rostam's father
- Relations: 
  - Father: Sam
  - Wife: Rudaba
  - Son: Rostam
  - Mentor: Simurgh
- Story Arc: Abandoned child becomes hero through Simurgh's aid
- Key Events:
  - Raised by Simurgh in Alborz Mountains
  - Romance with Rudaba (enemy princess)
  - Simurgh performs cesarean for Rostam's birth
- Dependencies: Bridge between Sam and Rostam
- **UNIQUE CHARACTER**: Magic connection to Simurgh

**RUDABA (Princess of Kabul)**
- Timeline: Same as Zal
- Role: Rostam's mother, bridge between enemies
- Relations: Daughter of Mehrab (Zahhak's descendant), wife of Zal
- Story Arc: Forbidden love, nearly dies giving birth to Rostam
- Dependencies: Romance subplot with Zal
- **STRONG FEMALE**: Agency in choosing spouse

## GAME NARRATIVE STRUCTURE RECOMMENDATIONS

### Option 1: Linear Multi-Generational Saga
**Pros:** Authentic to epic structure, shows passage of time
**Cons:** Players might lose attachment to early characters

**Structure:**
- **Act 1:** Mythical Age (Jamshid ‚Üí Zahhak ‚Üí Fereydun)
  - Playable: Fereydun, Kava
- **Act 2:** Rise of Heroes (Zal ‚Üí Young Rostam)
  - Playable: Zal, Young Rostam
- **Act 3:** Golden Age (Rostam's prime, Kay Kavus)
  - Playable: Rostam, Siavash
- **Act 4:** Tragedy (Sohrab, Kay Khosrow's revenge)
  - Playable: Sohrab, Kay Khosrow, Older Rostam
- **Act 5:** Twilight (Esfandiyar, Rostam's death)
  - Playable: Esfandiyar, Ancient Rostam

### Option 2: Rostam-Centric with Flashbacks
**Pros:** Single protagonist players bond with
**Cons:** Less variety, skips non-Rostam stories

**Structure:**
- Present: Old Rostam facing death
- Flashback to: Each major event as memory
- Unlock: Play as others in Rostam's memories
- Revelation: Understanding full story as death approaches

### Option 3: Anthology with Connecting Thread
**Pros:** Variety, each story self-contained
**Cons:** Less cohesive, harder to market

**Structure:**
- **Episode 1:** Fereydun vs Zahhak
- **Episode 2:** Rostam's Seven Trials  
- **Episode 3:** Rostam & Sohrab
- **Episode 4:** Siavash's Tragedy
- **Episode 5:** Kay Khosrow's Revenge
- **Episode 6:** Bizhan & Manizheh
- **Episode 7:** Esfandiyar vs Rostam
- **Connecting:** Simurgh as narrator/witness

### Option 4: Dual Timeline (RECOMMENDED FOR GAMES)
**Pros:** Variety + continuity, mystery/discovery
**Cons:** Complex to write

**Structure:**
**Timeline A (Past):** Play major events chronologically
**Timeline B (Present):** Modern-day descendant/scholar discovering truth
**Mechanic:** Past actions affect present understanding
**Narrative:** Parallel between ancient conflicts and modern struggles

**Example Flow:**
1. Present: Character inherits ancient text
2. Past: Play as Fereydun defeating Zahhak
3. Present: Discover family connection to heroes
4. Past: Play as young Rostam
5. Present: Face modern parallel to ancient conflict
6. Past: Play Rostam & Sohrab
7. Present: Understand cycle of tragedy
8. Resolution: Break or continue cycle

### Option 5: Branching Timeline (Maximum Replay)
**Pros:** Player agency, multiple endings
**Cons:** Huge development scope

**Structure:**
- Key decision points change history
- Example: Spare or kill Zahhak?
- Branching: Different heroes rise based on choices
- Multiple endings: Various fates for Persia

## MULTI-PERSPECTIVE NARRATIVE TECHNIQUES

### 1. **Character Shifting by Chapter**
- Chapter 1: Fereydun (hero POV)
- Chapter 2: Zahhak (villain POV of same events)
- Reveal: Different interpretations of morality

### 2. **Unreliable Narrators**
- Same event told by:
  - Iranian bard (pro-Iran)
  - Turanian bard (pro-Turan)
  - Simurgh (objective divine view)
- Player decides truth

### 3. **Parallel Lives**
- Split screen or alternating:
  - Rostam in Iran
  - Sohrab in Turan
  - Building to their fatal meeting

### 4. **Epistolary/Documents**
- Letters between characters
- Court records
- Prophecies
- Songs/poems
- Create layered understanding

### 5. **The Rashomon Effect**
- Rostam vs Sohrab fight
- Play as both
- Experience both perspectives
- No "correct" version

### 6. **Generational Echoes**
- Show same scenarios across generations:
  - Father/son conflicts (Sam/Zal, Rostam/Sohrab, Goshtasp/Esfandiyar)
  - Pattern recognition creates meaning
  - Player understands cycles

## CRITICAL PATH FOR GAME DEVELOPMENT

### Minimum Viable Epic (Year 1)
**Focus:** 3 interconnected stories
1. **Rostam's Seven Trials** (tutorial/combat)
2. **Rostam & Sohrab** (emotional peak)
3. **Kay Khosrow's Revenge** (conclusion)

**Why these?**
- Rostam as through-line
- Showcases variety (adventure, tragedy, war)
- Complete emotional arc
- Self-contained but expandable

### Full Saga (Year 2-3)
Add in order of priority:
1. Zahhak storyline (iconic villain)
2. Siavash tragedy (political intrigue)
3. Esfandiyar duel (ultimate challenge)
4. Early mythical kings (worldbuilding)
5. Historical epilogue (closure)

### DLC/Expansions
- Side stories (Bizhan & Manizheh)
- Alternative perspectives
- Historical Sasanian era
- "What if" scenarios
